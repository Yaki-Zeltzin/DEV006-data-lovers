HISTORY

Studio Ghibli was founded in 1985 by directors Hayao Miyazaki and Isao Takahata and producer Toshio Suzuki.

The pair first worked together in 1964, marking the beginning of a collaborative relationship that would carry on throughout their careers. In 1984, Miyazaki directed his first original feature film: Nausicaä of the Valley of the Wind.

Miyazaki and Suzuki decided to set up their own studio and invited Takahata to join them. So a journey was started that would result in 21 feature films and a reputation as one of the world’s best animation studios.
